Methodology

Our definition of poverty was described in the previous Topics (link below):
· https://elearning.uab.pt/mod/ forum/discuss.php?d=1042577# p3523263
· https://elearning.uab.pt/mod/ forum/discuss.php?d=1042577# p3526764

In general terms, we’ve defined poverty as being purely an economical condition (based on household net incomes).
  • For Developing countries, as stated by the United Nations: people living with less than 3.65$ daily - this adjusts for inflation - is considered to be living under the poverty line.
  • For EU27 Countries, EUROSTAT defines that anyone living with less than 60% of the countries’ median household net income, is below the poverty line.
  • Similarly, for the rest of the developed countries, the most common threshold for the poverty line is 50% of the countries’ median household net income.

    • We’ve based our methodology on the rules above.

    For this, several datasets have been collected and combined to generate this application:
  • href=https://ourworldindata.org/explorers/incomes-across-distribution-comparison -> ourworldindata - Dataset Incomes and Population Across the World;
  • https://eige.europa.eu/gender-statistics/dgs/indicator/ta_livcond_arop_gen__ilc_li02 -> European Institute of Gender Equality - At-risk-of-poverty rate by poverty threshold, age and sex (source: EU-SILC survey);
  • https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/databrowser/view/demo_pjan/default/table?lang=en&category=demo.demo_pop -> Eurostat - EU27 Countries Population by Gender
  • Bing Chat - List of LDC countries
