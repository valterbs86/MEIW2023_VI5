Methodology & App

Data collection / preparation

Our definition of poverty was described in the previous Topics (link below):
· https://elearning.uab.pt/mod/forum/discuss.php?d=1042577#p3523263
· https://elearning.uab.pt/mod/forum/discuss.php?d=1042577#p3526764

In general terms, we’ve defined poverty as being purely an economical condition (based on household net incomes).

For Developing countries, as stated by the United Nations: people living with less than 3.10$ a day (until 2016) or 3.65$ a day from 2017 onwards, is considered to be living in poverty (1.90$ and 2.15$ respectively are the thresholds for extreme poverty).
For EU27 Countries, EUROSTAT defines that anyone living with less than 60% of the countries’ median, is below the poverty line.
Similarly, for the rest of the developed countries, the most common threshold for the poverty line is 50% of the countries’ median.

We’ve based our methodology on the rules above.
For this, several datasets have been collected and combined to generate this application.

We’ve started by downloading a list of the World countries with the corresponding ISO2/ISO3 codes to facilitate the intersection between this list and other datasets (as very frequently countries are named differently in different sources). This list also includes Country Name and Continent.

Source:
· https://github.com/lukes/ISO-3166-Countries-with-Regional-Codes/blob/master/all/all.csv


Next, we’ve flagged the countries that are currently identified as Developing countries by the United Nations.

Source:
Bing Chat - Can you please list here all LDC countries


For the Incomes distribution, we’ve collected data from "ourworldindata", from where we could retrieve data for 41% of the countries in analysis.
From this dataset we managed to collect median net household incomes for several years and threshold daily income for each decile.
This allowed us to find an approximate percentage of population living below the poverty thresholds.

Source:
· https://ourworldindata.org/explorers/incomes-across-distribution-ppp2017?tab=table&time=1990..latest&country=~ALB&pickerSort=asc&pickerMetric=Entity&Indicator=Mean+income+or+consumption%2C+by+decile&Decile=All+deciles&Household+survey+data+type=Income+surveys+only&Period=Day&Show+breaks+between+less+comparable+surveys=false


For the missing countries, we’ve based ourselves on the Poverty headcount ratio at 2.15$ (for before 2017) or 3.65$ (for 2017 onwards) and the population count by year. (Population in extreme poverty).

Source:
· https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SI.POV.DDAY
· https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SI.POV.LMIC.GP
· https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SP.POP.TOTL


Lastly, we’ve collected data regarding the split of population by gender in EU27 and the median income by gender provided by "Eurostat". With this data we could estimate approximately the incidence of poverty by gender and how it affects differently the male and female population.

Source:
· https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/databrowser/view/ilc_di03/default/table?lang=en

*Observations:
Given the difficulties on finding public data on this domain, we have restricted our data collection only to 109 countries and the years between 2000 and 2022.
Additionally, all amounts are converted from USD to EUR at 0.91 exchange rate.

Details

This application contains 3 sheets with data.

Sheet 1 - World Overview - Population living under poverty line

5 visuals are available providing a Global overview on the World Population living under the Poverty or Extreme Poverty lines (as defined in the methodology).

Bar chart - Top 5 countries by continent sorted by percentage of population living under the poverty or extreme poverty lines.
World Map - population percentage living under the poverty or extreme poverty lines;
Bar chart - Average median daily Household Net Income (with a drill down by Continent and Country);
Mekko chart - Absolute contribution of each country to the Total number of people living under the poverty or extreme poverty lines;
Line chart - Timeline of Total Nr of people living under the poverty or extreme poverty lines by Continent;


*Observations:
For some countries in this sheet, we could only find data related to population living under Extreme Poverty lines, therefore figures might not be consistent.

In this sheet, the data is cross-sectional. Given the difficulties on finding data for all the years for all the countries, we choose to show the data for the latest year available for each country.

Additionally, some of the visuals are blocked, not responding to the selections made by the users. This was done on purpose so that the meaning of the visual is not lost.


Sheet 2 - EU27 Overview - Population living under poverty line
6 visuals are available providing a view on EU27 Member states population living under the poverty line (as defined in the methodology).

Bar chart - EU 27 Member States sorted by percentage of population living under the poverty line.
EU27 Map - percentage of population living under the poverty line;
Bar chart - EU27 countries Average median daily Household Net Income;
Mekko chart - Absolute contribution of the Member States to the Total number of people living under poverty line;
Mixed line / bar chart - Timeline of Total Nr of people living under the poverty line in EU27 with the incidence of poverty in the Male and Female population.
Pie chart - Split of poverty % per gender,

*Observations:
In this sheet the visuals are interactive, allowing selections and navigation.
Additionally, 2 drop down lists were added to allow for an easy selection of the year or country of analysis.

Sheet 3 - Tabular Data
Sheet containing a simple table with all dimensions and measures available in the applicaiton.
